Håller ni fortfarande på med allt manuellt?

Varje operations-team har samma problem. Dina system pratar inte med varandra. Dina medarbetare lägger timmar varje vecka på att kopiera data från ett ställe till ett annat. Saker missas. Fel smiter igenom. Och varje gång något nytt behöver automatiseras väntar du antingen på IT eller betalar en byrå.

Du har provat verktygen. "No-code"-byggarna kan antingen inte göra det du faktiskt behöver, eller så går de sönder så fort något går fel. Du slutar ändå med att stå och passa dem.

Flow80 är annorlunda. Det är byggt för riktigt operationsarbete — den sorten som faktiskt betyder något för din verksamhet. Det ansluter till systemen du redan använder, hanterar logiken, och kör pålitligt utan att du behöver bevaka det.

The honest comparison

The automation industry has a problem.

Every platform sells you the same promise: drag a few blocks, automate your whole operation, no code required. That promise holds for exactly as long as your workflow stays a two-step toy example.

Platform The verdict The proof
Zapier
Best-in-class first-run experience — that falls apart the moment your workflow does.
The 2-step Zap takes 15–30 minutes. Zapier's own learning platform is 36 hours of content. That's how much complexity they're hiding.
Make
The most powerful template library in the category, buried under a UI that takes 1–3 months to navigate confidently.
7,500+ pre-built templates exist. Community reports put actual automation proficiency at 1–3 months of practice.
n8n
Open-source flexibility that assumes you have a developer on staff to maintain it.
60% of non-technical users abandon n8n before reaching a working workflow. Docker and JSON expressions are the documented failure points.
Power Automate
Microsoft's answer to workflow automation, buried under licensing complexity and a UI users are actively fleeing.
The early-2026 UI redesign is the subject of widespread Reddit criticism. "Gave up" posts are routine.
UiPath
Enterprise-grade RPA built for a team of engineers, not an ops manager with a problem to solve this week.
Getting a reliable business process running takes 2–6 weeks of learning. Recovery-from-failure is a documented pain point even for trained users.
The difference that matters
Competitors win the first 20 minutes.
Flow80 wins the first 20 hours.
When Zapier silently fails a webhook overnight. When n8n starts throwing JSON errors. When UiPath needs a developer to fix the unattended bot. That's when automation actually matters — and that's exactly where Flow80 was designed to work.
Flow80 was built with a different assumption: that ops work matters most when things go wrong at 2am. Circuit breakers. Per-job MongoDB logging. Automatic retry logic. A failing job tells you exactly what broke and why — it doesn't silently disappear.
